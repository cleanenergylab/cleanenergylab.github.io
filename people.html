Dr. Yingguang Zhang
Yingguang joined the group as a PhD student in September 2018, studying the photocatalysis for pollute degradation. She is current a postdoc in the group. Her background is in Chemistry Engineering, she carried out a MEng Engineering degree at Fuzhou University in 2017.
Dr. Xiaolong Zhao
Xiaolong Zhao is a postdoctoral fellow in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Hong Kong. He obtained his PhD degree from the Department of Mechanical Engineering in 2022 from the University of Hong Kong. His research interests include nanomaterial fabrication, photo (electro) catalytic energy conversion and mechanism study.
Shijing Luo
Shijing Luo has a strong academic background in material science and mechanical engineering. As a Ph.D. student at HKU, Shijing has been strengthening her research skills in mechanical engineering and electrochemistry. With interests in energy conversion and energy storage, she is gaining expertise in developing high-performance, portable, and flexible energy devices such as fuel cells and batteries. One of the flexible fuel cell projects has been awarded the 3rd prize in the 7th HK Innovation Competition. To broaden her knowledge in other fields, Shijing also minored in Accounting as a second degree during her undergraduate study at Harbin Institute of Technology and was awarded a degree of the Bachelor of Management. Meanwhile, Shijing has been actively participating in extracurricular activities. She is now serving the management team as the student representative in one of the HKU Accommodation Halls, assisting in administrative work and all kinds of student events. She was also one of the co-founders of Transmission Childhood Education Foundation (China Northeastern Branch), a non-profit organization that provides diverse educational opportunities and passes a better childhood for children in poverty. After organizing some summer camps of short-term education for pupils in the rural area and working as a lead teacher, she was also awarded as “Excellent Organizer and Staff” in 2013.
Sarah K.W. Leong
Sarah is a current Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Mechanical Engineering with the Hong Kong PhD Fellowship. Before joining the Lab, she has two years of project management experience at an engineering consultancy firm in California, USA. She got her Master of Engineering degree in University of California, Berkeley and Bachelor of Science degree in Columbia University..
Jessica Mingyu Xia
Mingyu Xia received her M.S. degree in Chemistry from the Imperial College London, the United Kingdom in 2021, and is currently pursuing her Ph.D. degree in mechanical engineering, the University of Hong Kong. Her research interests focus on photocatalysis and solar cells.
